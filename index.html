    ‚ÑπÔ∏è Theory & Applications

    • Friction: Rubbing transfers electrons from one material to another. The one gaining electrons becomes negative; the other positive.
    • Induction: A nearby charged object redistributes charges in a conductor. With grounding, electrons can leave/enter, leaving a net charge after disconnect.
    • Applications: Balloons sticking to walls, photocopiers/laser printers, powder coating, Van de Graaff generators.
