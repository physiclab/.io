    ℹ️ Theory & Applications

    📚 Physics Theory

    • Linear Expansion: ΔL = L₀ × α × ΔT
    • Areal Expansion: ΔA = A₀ × 2α × ΔT
    • Volumetric Expansion: ΔV = V₀ × 3α × ΔT
    • α = coefficient of linear expansion
    • ΔT = temperature change

    🏗️ Real-world Applications

    • Railway Tracks: Expansion joints prevent buckling
    • Bridges: Thermal expansion joints
    • Thermometers: Mercury/glass expansion
    • Bimetallic Strips: Different expansion rates
    • Pipelines: Expansion loops and joints

    ℹ️ Theory & Applications

    📚 Fourier’s Law

    • Q = k · A · (T₁ - T₂) / d

    ℹ️ Theory & Applications

    • Friction: Rubbing transfers electrons from one material to another. The one gaining electrons becomes negative; the other positive.
    • Induction: A nearby charged object redistributes charges in a conductor. With grounding, electrons can leave/enter, leaving a net charge after disconnect.
    • Applications: Balloons sticking to walls, photocopiers/laser printers, powder coating, Van de Graaff generators.

    ℹ️ Theory & Applications

    • In parallel circuits, voltage across each branch equals the source voltage.
    • Current divides among branches: Ii = V/Ri (switch ON).
    • Equivalent resistance: 1/Req = Σ(1/Ri); Itotal = ΣIi.
    • Applications: household wiring, power strips, car electrical systems.

    0.00 W
    Formulas
    Rtotal = ΣRi, I = V/Rtotal, Vi = I×Ri, P = VI

    Resistor Information

    ℹ️ Theory & Applications

    • In series circuits, current is the same through all components: I₁ = I₂ = I₃ = Itotal
    • Total resistance: Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ... (resistances add up)
    • Voltage drops: V₁ = I×R₁, V₂ = I×R₂, and V₁ + V₂ + ... = Vbattery
    • Applications: Christmas lights, flashlights, voltage dividers, current limiting
